# T25 – Data Representation & Simple Data Types: K–8 Skill List (Draft v1)

Topic reference: `T25 Data Representation & Simple Data Types` in `domains_topics_overview.md`
Domain: Data & Analysis (D3) · CSTA focus: DAA‑DF (Data Fundamentals, with links to PRO‑DH, DAA‑DP/DI)

Each skill below has:

- a stable **ID** (`T25.G<grade>.<nn>`),
- an IXL‑style **short name**,
- a **description** (what understanding/behavior it targets),
- a **challenge format** (the typical problem type to assess it).

Where relevant, a primary **CSTA code** is noted.

---

## Grade K (PreK–K)

In Kindergarten, students develop foundational understanding of data as "information" and recognize that different types of information exist around them. The focus is on concrete, visual recognition rather than formal coding.

### T25.GK.01 – Spot what's a number vs. other information

- **Short name:** Identify numbers vs. pictures/words
- **Description:** Students distinguish between different types of data they encounter daily (e.g., a picture of a cat, the word "cat," and the number "3"). They learn that numbers are one way to represent information, but not the only way.
- **Challenge format:** Concept, multiple choice or sorting. Show a collection of objects (pictures, written words, numerals, symbols) and ask students to group them by type or select "which one is a number?" Auto‑grading checks correct identification.
- **CSTA:** EK‑DAA‑DF‑01 (Demonstrate how people create and collect various types of data).

### T25.GK.02 – Count objects and match to numerals

- **Short name:** Match quantities to numbers
- **Description:** Students count physical or pictured objects and connect the quantity to a numeral (1, 2, 3, etc.), understanding that a numeral represents "how many." This bridges everyday counting to formal numeric data.
- **Challenge format:** Concept, interactive activity. Display a group of identical objects (e.g., apples, blocks). Students count them and select or type the matching number. Auto‑grading compares the chosen numeral to the actual count.
- **CSTA:** EK‑DAA‑DF‑01.

### T25.GK.03 – Recognize color, shape, or size categories

- **Short name:** Sort by color, shape, or size
- **Description:** Students identify and group objects by a shared attribute (e.g., all red objects, all circles, all big items), laying the groundwork for understanding categorical data.
- **Challenge format:** Concept, interactive sorting. Provide a mix of objects with different colors, shapes, or sizes. Students drag items into labeled bins (e.g., "Red" and "Not Red") or circle all items of one category. Auto‑grading checks groupings.
- **CSTA:** EK‑DAA‑DF‑01.

### T25.GK.04 – Use symbols to represent simple information

- **Short name:** Draw or pick a symbol for an idea
- **Description:** Students explore the idea that a simple symbol (smiley face, star, thumbs up) can stand for information (happy, important, good). This introduces the concept of encoding and representation.
- **Challenge format:** Concept, guided activity. Provide a simple key (e.g., smiley = happy, frown = sad) and ask students to draw or select the symbol that matches a given feeling or idea. Auto‑grading scores symbolic accuracy.
- **CSTA:** EK‑PRO‑DH‑03 (Identify gestures and symbols that represent information).

## Grade 1

Grade 1 students expand their understanding of data types to include numbers and non‑numeric information (names, colors, yes/no). They start to understand that data can be collected and organized.

### T25.G1.01 – Collect and sort data by simple categories

- **Short name:** Collect and organize data
- **Description:** Students gather simple data from classmates or classroom objects (e.g., favorite colors, pets owned, breakfast foods) and organize it into categories or groups, recognizing that data can be organized in different ways.
- **Challenge format:** Coding or concept, guided project. In a CreatiCode project, students use simple input blocks to ask a question (e.g., "Do you like cats or dogs?") and store responses in categories. Auto‑grading checks that responses are grouped correctly.
- **CSTA:** E1‑DAA‑DF‑01 (Compare numeric and non‑numeric data in terms of how they are collected).

### T25.G1.02 – Compare numbers as "more," "less," or "same"

- **Short name:** Compare quantities
- **Description:** Students compare two groups of objects or two numeric values using words like "more," "fewer," and "equal," developing a sense of numeric comparison.
- **Challenge format:** Concept, multiple choice or interactive. Show two groups of objects or two numbers; students select which has more, which has less, or if they are the same. Auto‑grading checks selections.
- **CSTA:** E1‑DAA‑DF‑01.

### T25.G1.03 – Recognize that numbers have special meaning

- **Short name:** Understand what numbers tell us
- **Description:** Students learn that numbers convey specific information (age, count, score, temperature) and that the same numeral can mean different things in different contexts (3 years old vs. score of 3).
- **Challenge format:** Concept, guided activity. Present pictures or scenarios where numbers appear (e.g., "I am 6 years old," "The score is 10"). Students match the number to what it describes or explain what it means. Auto‑grading checks explanations or matches.
- **CSTA:** E1‑DAA‑DF‑01.

### T25.G1.04 – Create a simple tally or picture chart

- **Short name:** Make a chart of information
- **Description:** Students create a simple visual record of collected data (tally marks, pictures in rows) to show the information more clearly. This introduces the idea of data visualization.
- **Challenge format:** Coding, guided project. In a CreatiCode project with pre‑drawn chart outlines, students drag or place sprite stamps to create a bar‑like chart showing counted data. Auto‑grading checks that all collected items are represented.
- **CSTA:** E1‑DAA‑DF‑01.

## Grade 2

Grade 2 students work with multiple data types and begin to understand the difference between counting data and categorical data. They use variables for the first time.

### T25.G2.01 – Compare numeric and non‑numeric data types

- **Short name:** What data type is this?
- **Description:** Students identify and classify data as either numeric (numbers: age, score, quantity) or non‑numeric/categorical (words or categories: color, name, type of food).
- **Challenge format:** Concept, multiple choice or sorting. Present a list or collection of data items. Students categorize each as "number data" or "word/category data." Auto‑grading checks classifications.
- **CSTA:** E2‑DAA‑DF‑01 (Collect numeric and non‑numeric data using multiple methods).

### T25.G2.02 – Create a variable to store a single piece of data

- **Short name:** Make a variable and store a value
- **Description:** Students learn to create a CreatiCode variable (e.g., `my score`, `favorite color`) and assign it a value, understanding that a variable is a named container for information.
- **Challenge format:** Coding, starter project. Prompt: "Create a variable called 'score' and set it to 10." Students create the variable and initialize its value. Auto‑grading checks for correct variable creation and initialization.
- **CSTA:** E2‑PRO‑DH‑02 (Label different representations of information with a name and whether its value is constant or changes).

### T25.G2.03 – Identify when data is numeric vs. constant

- **Short name:** Does this value change?
- **Description:** Students distinguish between constant values (e.g., a name, a fixed color choice) and values that change over time (e.g., score, age, temperature).
- **Challenge format:** Concept, multiple choice. Present scenarios or information and ask students to pick whether each represents a changing or constant value. Auto‑grading checks answers.
- **CSTA:** E2‑PRO‑DH‑02.

### T25.G2.04 – Use a variable to track changing information

- **Short name:** Update a variable's value
- **Description:** Students modify a variable's value as a program runs (e.g., increasing a score, updating a timer), understanding that variables allow us to keep track of information that changes.
- **Challenge format:** Coding, starter project. Provided: a sprite and a variable (e.g., `lives`). Prompt: "When the player touches a coin, increase `lives` by 1." Auto‑grading checks that the variable updates correctly.
- **CSTA:** E2‑PRO‑DH‑02.

### T25.G2.05 – Organize collected data into a simple table

- **Short name:** Put data in a table
- **Description:** Students collect data (e.g., students' favorite foods) and organize it into a simple two‑column table (item and count or name and choice), understanding tables as an organized way to show data.
- **Challenge format:** Coding or concept, guided activity. In CreatiCode or on paper, students create a table with labeled columns and enter collected data. Auto‑grading checks table structure and data accuracy.
- **CSTA:** E2‑DAA‑DF‑01.

## Grade 3

Grade 3 introduces more formal understanding of data types and the idea of reliable data collection. Students also encounter booleans informally.

### T25.G3.01 – Understand accuracy and completeness in data

- **Short name:** Is this data correct and complete?
- **Description:** Students evaluate collected data to check if it is accurate (correct values) and complete (no missing entries), learning that data quality matters.
- **Challenge format:** Concept, guided analysis. Show a small dataset with some errors (missing entries, unclear labels, wrong counts) and ask students to identify what is wrong and how to fix it. Auto‑grading checks explanations.
- **CSTA:** E3‑DAA‑DF‑02 (Evaluate collected data for accuracy and completeness).

### T25.G3.02 – Create a survey and collect data systematically

- **Short name:** Design and run a survey
- **Description:** Students plan a simple survey (picking a clear question, deciding on answer choices), run it with classmates, and collect the responses systematically.
- **Challenge format:** Coding, guided project. In CreatiCode, students use input blocks to ask a survey question and store results in variables or lists. The project includes data entry for 5–10 respondents. Auto‑grading checks that question and answers are recorded properly.
- **CSTA:** E3‑DAA‑DF‑01 (Collaborate with peers to collect numeric and non‑numeric data).

### T25.G3.03 – Distinguish between different data types in a program

- **Short name:** Name the data types in code
- **Description:** Students read a simple program and identify the data types of variables used (e.g., `score` is a number, `name` is text), understanding that different kinds of information need different storage.
- **Challenge format:** Concept, code‑reading item. Show a CreatiCode project with several variables. Students label each variable as "number," "text," "true/false," or "list." Auto‑grading checks classifications.
- **CSTA:** E3‑PRO‑DH‑02 (Identify the variables being stored and manipulated in a program).

### T25.G3.04 – Use true/false values to record simple facts

- **Short name:** Store yes/no answers
- **Description:** Students create or use a variable that stores a true/false (boolean) value, such as `is playing` or `has coin`, learning that some information is naturally yes/no.
- **Challenge format:** Coding, starter project. Prompt: "Create a variable called 'is jumping'. Set it to true when the player jumps, and false when they land." Auto‑grading checks for correct boolean assignment.
- **CSTA:** E3‑PRO‑DH‑02.

### T25.G3.05 – Build a data structure with a list

- **Short name:** Create and use a list
- **Description:** Students create a list (or array) in CreatiCode and add items to it (e.g., a list of high scores, a list of item names), understanding lists as a way to store multiple related values.
- **Challenge format:** Coding, starter project. Provided: a list with a few starting values. Prompt: "Add 3 more scores to the list." Or: "Create a list of 5 favorite foods." Auto‑grading checks that the list is created/modified correctly.
- **CSTA:** E3‑PRO‑DH‑02, DAA‑DF.

## Grade 4

Grade 4 students formalize their understanding of data types and begin to use data structures (lists, tables) to solve real problems. Binary encoding is introduced.

### T25.G4.01 – Represent data in a table with rows and columns

- **Short name:** Organize data in a table
- **Description:** Students create or work with tables where rows represent individual records (e.g., a person) and columns represent attributes (e.g., name, age, favorite color). This is foundational for understanding structured data.
- **Challenge format:** Coding, guided project. In CreatiCode, students organize survey or game data using a table variable (or list of lists). Prompt: "Create a table with columns for 'Player Name' and 'Score'." Auto‑grading checks table structure.
- **CSTA:** E4‑DAA‑DF‑01 (Organize data into tables where rows represent a record and columns represent attributes).

### T25.G4.02 – Understand simple binary representation

- **Short name:** Encode information as on/off
- **Description:** Students explore the idea that information can be encoded using just two states (on/off, 1/0, true/false), laying groundwork for understanding how computers store data.
- **Challenge format:** Concept, interactive activity. Show a simple binary code key (e.g., "1 = yes, 0 = no"). Students decode a short binary sequence to reveal a message or answer a question. Auto‑grading checks decoded answers.
- **CSTA:** EK‑PRO‑DH‑03 (adapted for Grade 4).

### T25.G4.03 – Trace how data flows through a program

- **Short name:** Follow data through a program
- **Description:** Students read a short program and trace how a piece of data (e.g., user input, a variable) enters, is modified, and produces an output, understanding data as information flowing through code.
- **Challenge format:** Concept, code‑tracing item. Show a CreatiCode script with input, variable updates, and output. Ask: "What is the value of `score` after this script runs?" Auto‑grading checks final values or outputs.
- **CSTA:** E4‑PRO‑DH‑02 (Trace how data flows through and alters values in an existing program).

### T25.G4.04 – Store and retrieve data from a list by position

- **Short name:** Access items in a list
- **Description:** Students use list operations to add, remove, or access items at specific positions in a list, understanding lists as ordered collections.
- **Challenge format:** Coding, starter project. Provided: a list of items. Prompt: "Get the 3rd item in the list and display it." Or: "Add a new item at the end of the list." Auto‑grading checks code correctness and output.
- **CSTA:** E4‑PRO‑DH‑02.

### T25.G4.05 – Convert between data representations

- **Short name:** Change how data is displayed
- **Description:** Students transform data from one format to another (e.g., from a numeric count to a word description, from individual values to a list) to use information in different ways.
- **Challenge format:** Coding or concept, guided activity. Example: "You have 3 coins. Show this as the text 'You have 3 coins' and also as a list [coin, coin, coin]." Auto‑grading checks both representations.
- **CSTA:** E4‑PRO‑DH‑02, DAA‑DP.

## Grade 5

Grade 5 students work with multiple data types in combination and use CreatiCode's table variables for more complex projects. The connection between data and programs becomes explicit.

### T25.G5.01 – Use multiple data types in a single program

- **Short name:** Mix number, text, boolean, and list types
- **Description:** Students design a program that uses several data types together (e.g., player name as text, score as a number, items collected as a list, is playing as boolean) to represent a complete scenario.
- **Challenge format:** Coding, design challenge. Prompt: "Create a program that tracks a player with a name (text), score (number), and items in inventory (list). Initialize all variables." Auto‑grading checks for all required types.
- **CSTA:** E5‑PRO‑DH‑02 (Use different types of variables to store, compare, and modify data).

### T25.G5.02 – Create and manipulate a table of records

- **Short name:** Use a table to store structured data
- **Description:** Students use CreatiCode's table variable to store multiple records (e.g., a leaderboard with names and scores, a game inventory with items and quantities), accessing and updating specific fields.
- **Challenge format:** Coding, guided project. Provided: empty table structure. Prompt: "Add 3 players with names and starting scores. Update the first player's score." Auto‑grading checks table content and updates.
- **CSTA:** E5‑DAA‑DF‑01, DAA‑DP.

### T25.G5.03 – Understand different ways to encode the same data

- **Short name:** Binary, decimal, and symbol encoding
- **Description:** Students explore how the same information can be represented in different ways (e.g., the number 5 in decimal, 101 in binary, or ***** as a tally), understanding encoding choices.
- **Challenge format:** Concept, interactive activity. Show a piece of information (e.g., "5 stars collected") and ask students to encode it in three different ways (tally, binary, words). Auto‑grading checks all three representations.
- **CSTA:** E5‑PRO‑DH‑02, DAA‑DF.

### T25.G5.04 – Design a simple data collection system

- **Short name:** Plan data collection for a project
- **Description:** Students design what data they need to collect for a project (e.g., game times, player choices, sensor readings) and plan how to store and organize it.
- **Challenge format:** Coding or concept, design task. Prompt: "You're making a quiz game. Decide what data to store (player name, score, time taken, etc.) and how to organize it in variables and lists." Auto‑grading checks for completeness and appropriateness of choices.
- **CSTA:** E5‑DAA‑DF‑01.

### T25.G5.05 – Convert a program to use lists instead of multiple variables

- **Short name:** Refactor using lists or tables
- **Description:** Students take a program that uses many similar variables (e.g., `score1`, `score2`, `score3`) and refactor it to use a single list or table instead, reducing duplication and improving clarity.
- **Challenge format:** Coding, refactor challenge. Starter project uses 5 separate number variables for player scores. Prompt: "Rewrite this using a single list called `scores`." Auto‑grading checks that the refactored version produces the same results with cleaner code.
- **CSTA:** E5‑PRO‑DH‑02, DAA‑DF.

## Grade 6

In middle school, students develop more sophisticated understanding of data types and work with both local and cloud-based data storage. They begin to think about data metadata and quality.

### T25.G6.01 – Examine data types and their properties

- **Short name:** Identify properties of different data types
- **Description:** Students understand what values each data type can hold and why certain types are appropriate for certain tasks (numbers for quantities, text for names, booleans for states, lists for collections).
- **Challenge format:** Concept, multiple choice or explanation. Present scenarios (e.g., "store a player's age") and ask students to choose the best data type and explain why. Auto‑grading checks choices and reasoning.
- **CSTA:** MS‑PRO‑DH‑05 (Use appropriate data types to store, update, and evaluate data).

### T25.G6.02 – Work with local and cloud-backed variables

- **Short name:** Understand local vs. shared variables
- **Description:** Students learn that variables can be local (only within a sprite or script) or shared/cloud-backed (accessible to multiple sprites or across sessions), understanding when each type is appropriate.
- **Challenge format:** Concept, guided analysis or coding. Show a scenario (e.g., a two-player game) and ask whether the data should be local or shared. Students make a choice and justify it. Auto‑grading checks appropriateness.
- **CSTA:** MS‑PRO‑DH‑04 (Represent data using appropriate data structures).

### T25.G6.03 – Create metadata describing a dataset

- **Short name:** Document what your data means
- **Description:** Students write simple metadata for a dataset they collect (e.g., "This list contains student names from Grade 6. Collected on [date]"). This prepares them to understand data context.
- **Challenge format:** Concept or coding, guided activity. Prompt: "You collected favorite colors from 20 classmates. Write 2–3 sentences explaining what this data is, when you collected it, and who contributed." Auto‑grading checks for key metadata elements.
- **CSTA:** MS‑DAA‑DF‑04 (Create metadata that describes the purpose and contents of datasets).

### T25.G6.04 – Filter and organize data using computational tools

- **Short name:** Sort and filter data
- **Description:** Students use CreatiCode blocks (or similar tools) to filter a list or table (e.g., show only high scores, show only red items) and sort data (e.g., alphabetical, numerical), understanding how the same data can be viewed in different ways.
- **Challenge format:** Coding, starter project. Provided: a table or list of data. Prompt: "Filter the list to show only items worth more than 5 points, then sort by name." Auto‑grading checks filtered and sorted output.
- **CSTA:** MS‑DAA‑DP‑05 (Use computational tools to organize, filter, group, and aggregate data).

### T25.G6.05 – Distinguish between nominal, ordinal, and numeric data

- **Short name:** Categorize data by how it's organized
- **Description:** Students classify data as nominal (categories with no order, like colors), ordinal (categories with order, like T-shirt sizes), or numeric (continuous or discrete numbers), understanding how data type affects analysis.
- **Challenge format:** Concept, sorting or multiple choice. Present data examples (e.g., "shirt sizes," "temperatures," "animal types") and ask students to classify them. Auto‑grading checks classifications.
- **CSTA:** MS‑DAA‑DF‑01 (Examine the relationship between data and metadata).

## Grade 7

Grade 7 students deepen their work with data types in realistic projects and begin to reason about the limitations and implications of data representation choices.

### T25.G7.01 – Represent numbers at different precision levels

- **Short name:** Understand precision in numeric data
- **Description:** Students explore how the precision of numeric data affects storage and meaning (e.g., rounding 3.14159 to 3.14, storing temperature to the nearest degree vs. tenth), understanding trade-offs.
- **Challenge format:** Concept or coding, analysis task. Show a numeric value and ask: "If you store this as a whole number instead of a decimal, what information do you lose?" Students explain. Auto‑grading scores explanations.
- **CSTA:** MS‑DAA‑DF‑02 (Evaluate the impact of precision and granularity in data collection).

### T25.G7.02 – Use nested data structures to represent complex information

- **Short name:** Store complex data in lists and tables
- **Description:** Students create or work with nested structures (e.g., a list of tables, where each table represents a player with multiple attributes) to model real-world complexity.
- **Challenge format:** Coding, design challenge. Prompt: "Create a list of teams. Each team should have a name, a list of 3 player names, and a score." Auto‑grading checks structure correctness.
- **CSTA:** MS‑PRO‑DH‑04, MS‑DAA‑DP‑05.

### T25.G7.03 – Evaluate the appropriateness of a data type for a task

- **Short name:** Pick the right data type
- **Description:** Students analyze a project scenario and make evidence-based decisions about what data types to use, considering what operations will be needed and what values are expected.
- **Challenge format:** Concept or coding, design task. Present a scenario (e.g., "Make a high score tracker"). Ask: "What data types should you use, and why?" Students justify their choices. Auto‑grading checks reasoning.
- **CSTA:** MS‑PRO‑DH‑05.

### T25.G7.04 – Handle missing or invalid data in a program

- **Short name:** Deal with incomplete or wrong data
- **Description:** Students write code that gracefully handles missing values (e.g., a player with no high score) or invalid input, understanding data quality issues.
- **Challenge format:** Coding, defensive programming task. Prompt: "Write code that asks for a player's age. If they enter a negative number or leave it blank, show an error message instead of storing it." Auto‑grading checks error handling.
- **CSTA:** MS‑DAA‑DP‑07 (Identify errors in data and ways to fix them).

### T25.G7.05 – Compare storage and efficiency of different data representations

- **Short name:** Choose efficient ways to store data
- **Description:** Students compare two ways of representing the same information (e.g., storing 5 items as individual variables vs. a list, or as a single tally number) and reason about efficiency and clarity.
- **Challenge format:** Concept or analysis, code-reading task. Show two programs storing similar data differently and ask: "Which one uses fewer variables? Which is easier to update?" Auto‑grading checks reasoning.
- **CSTA:** MS‑DAA‑DF‑02, MS‑PRO‑DH‑04.

## Grade 8

Grade 8 students apply sophisticated data type reasoning to real projects and consider the societal implications of data representation choices.

### T25.G8.01 – Design a complete data schema for a project

- **Short name:** Plan your data structure
- **Description:** Students design a detailed plan for what data a project will store, including all variable types, relationships, and constraints (e.g., "score must be non-negative," "player name is required").
- **Challenge format:** Coding or concept, design document task. Prompt: "You're making a multi-player quiz game. Document all the data you need: player records (name, score, completion status), quiz questions, and user settings. Define each variable type." Auto‑grading checks completeness and consistency.
- **CSTA:** MS‑DAA‑DF‑03, MS‑DAA‑DF‑04 (Use computational tools and create metadata).

### T25.G8.02 – Recognize bias and limitations in data representation

- **Short name:** Think critically about how data is encoded
- **Description:** Students explore how choices in data representation can introduce bias or lose important information (e.g., representing gender as only "boy" or "girl," categorizing ages into broad ranges, using single-language labels).
- **Challenge format:** Concept, critical analysis. Present a data schema or survey design with limitations and ask: "Who might not be represented well by this design? How would you improve it?" Auto‑grading scores thoughtful explanations.
- **CSTA:** MS‑DAA‑IM‑14 (Analyze how decisions during data collection can lead to biased data).

### T25.G8.03 – Use advanced data structures for algorithms

- **Short name:** Choose data structures for efficiency
- **Description:** Students select appropriate data structures (lists, tables, or dictionaries) for an algorithmic task, understanding that the choice affects how efficiently the program can search, sort, or process data.
- **Challenge format:** Coding, algorithm design task. Prompt: "Write code to find a player by name in a list of 100 players. Why would a list work? Would a table (name + score + status) be better?" Auto‑grading checks both the working code and reasoning.
- **CSTA:** MS‑PRO‑DH‑04, MS‑PRO‑DH‑05.

### T25.G8.04 – Explore how data type mismatches cause errors

- **Short name:** Debug data type errors
- **Description:** Students encounter and fix programs where incorrect operations are performed on wrong data types (e.g., adding the text "5" to the number 10, or trying to sort mixed data types incorrectly), understanding type constraints.
- **Challenge format:** Coding, debugging challenge. Starter project contains a bug where incompatible data types are used together (e.g., trying to increment a string or access a list index with text). Students identify and fix the issue. Auto‑grading checks that the error is resolved.
- **CSTA:** MS‑PRO‑TR‑11 (Use standard practices to test, debug, and peer-review code).

### T25.G8.05 – Evaluate how data representation affects real-world decisions

- **Short name:** Understand data representation's impact
- **Description:** Students analyze real-world scenarios where data representation choices have consequences (e.g., how credit scores are calculated, how student grades are recorded, how census categories are defined) and discuss ethical implications.
- **Challenge format:** Concept, discussion or short paper. Prompt: "Imagine a store's loyalty program that tracks customer purchases. What data should it store, and what might be misused? How could the design be more ethical?" Auto‑grading scores depth of reasoning.
- **CSTA:** MS‑DAA‑IM‑13, MS‑DAA‑IM‑14 (Impacts of Data Science).

---

## Summary Progression

- **K–1:** Foundational recognition of numeric vs. non-numeric information; first exposure to symbols and categories.
- **2–3:** Introduction to variables, basic data types, simple lists, and structured collection methods.
- **4–5:** Working with tables, multiple data types in combination, binary ideas, and refactoring using appropriate structures.
- **6–7:** Cloud and local variables, metadata, filtering and sorting, nested structures, and efficiency considerations.
- **8:** Advanced schema design, bias in representation, data-driven algorithms, type safety, and societal implications.

---

## Alignment Notes

- **T25 to PRO-DH:** Data handling in programming (variables, lists, type choices) is essential to using data effectively in code.
- **T25 to DAA-DF:** Data Fundamentals standards directly align with understanding different data types, how data is collected, and metadata.
- **T25 to DAA-DP:** Data Processing standards depend on correct initial choice and representation of data types.
- **T25 to DAA-DI:** Data Investigations require students to think carefully about what data to collect and how to organize it.

All skills in this document support the foundational principle that effective computing requires understanding how to represent information digitally—from simple numbers and text to complex, multi-part data structures.
