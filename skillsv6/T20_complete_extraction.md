### T20.GK.01
**Topic:** T20 – AI Media
**Skill:** Identify which picture looks computer-made vs real
**Description:** **Student task:** Look at 4 pairs of pictures. For each pair, tap the picture that looks like a computer made it. **Visual scenario:** Pair 1: Real photo of a golden retriever vs AI image of dog with slightly blurry fur and odd shadow. Pair 2: Real photo of a birthday cake vs AI cake with melting candles and wobbly text. Pair 3: Real sunset photo vs AI sunset with impossible cloud shapes. Pair 4: Real classroom photo vs AI classroom where some children have 6 fingers. After each selection, a green checkmark appears on correct answers with a hint bubble: "Good eye! The computer picture has [specific clue]." **Key insight:** AI pictures often have small mistakes that real cameras don't make.
**Activity Type:** Picture comparison with tap-to-select
**Estimated Time:** 3-4 minutes
**CSTA:** 1A-IC-16 (Compare how people lived and worked before and with technology)
**Dependencies:** None

## GRADE 3 (8 skills)

### T20.G3.01
**Topic:** T20 – AI Media
**Skill:** Distinguish AI-generated media from recorded media
**Description:** Students analyze 6 pairs of media (3 image pairs, 3 audio pairs) and identify which is AI-generated vs recorded/photographed. For images, they look for: unnatural symmetry, impossible shadows, texture repetition, artifact hands/faces, gibberish text. For audio, they listen for: robotic tone, unnatural breathing pauses, too-perfect pronunciation, mechanical rhythm. Students explain their reasoning for each choice and build a "detection checklist" of AI clues. This foundational skill builds critical media literacy for an AI-saturated world.
**CSTA:** 2-IC-20
**Dependencies:**
* T20.G2.06

### T20.G3.02
**Topic:** T20 – AI Media
**Skill:** Construct AI image prompts using a structured template
**Description:** Students learn the [SUBJECT] + [DESCRIPTION] + [SETTING] + [STYLE] prompt template. They transform vague requests into structured prompts: "I want a cat picture" → "orange tabby cat with green eyes, sitting on a blue velvet couch, in a sunny living room, cartoon style." Students complete 5 templates, then rate prompts on a 1-5 specificity scale and predict which will produce better AI results. They identify and fix 3 "too vague" prompts (e.g., "something cool" → structured version). This template-based thinking prepares for actual AI image generation blocks in Grade 5.
**CSTA:** 2-IC-20
**Dependencies:**
* T20.G3.01
* T20.G2.01

### T20.G3.03
**Topic:** T20 – AI Media
**Skill:** Evaluate AI image outputs against requirements
**Description:** Students receive a prompt ("friendly robot helper in a classroom, blue and silver colors, cartoon style") and 6 AI-generated images. They sort each into: "Ready to Use" (matches all requirements), "Fixable" (close but missing something), or "Unusable" (major problems). For each image, they complete a checklist: □ Has robot? □ In classroom? □ Blue/silver colors? □ Cartoon style? □ Looks friendly? □ No major artifacts? Students justify each rating and identify what would need to change for "Fixable" images. This builds systematic evaluation skills for later prompt iteration workflows.
**Activity Type:** Rubric-based evaluation with justification
**Estimated Time:** 5-6 minutes
**CSTA:** 2-IC-20
**Dependencies:**
* T20.G3.02
* T20.G2.05
