## T01 - Everyday Algorithms (8 skills)

### T01.GK.01
**Title:** Put pictures in order for getting ready for bed
**Dependencies:** None
**Description:** Look at 3 pictures. Put them in order: first, next, last. Example: Put on pajamas, brush teeth, get in bed. Drag-drop sequence (3 items).

### T01.GK.02
**Title:** Put pictures in order for coming to class
**Dependencies:** None
**Description:** Look at 4 pictures. Put them in the right order. Example: Come in, hang up backpack, sit down, listen. Drag-drop 4 items.

### T01.GK.03
**Title:** Find the first and last pictures
**Dependencies:** T01.GK.02
**Description:** Look at pictures in order. Touch the first picture. Touch the last picture. Click first/last pictures.

### T01.GK.04
**Title:** Pick the pictures that make sense
**Dependencies:** T01.GK.01
**Description:** Look at two sets of pictures. Which one makes sense? Example: Wash hands → dry hands → eat vs eat → wash hands → dry hands. Two sequences, choose one.

### T01.GK.05
**Title:** Move the picture that's in the wrong place
**Dependencies:** T01.GK.03
**Description:** Look at the pictures. One picture is in the wrong place. Move it to the right place. Drag the "wrong" card into correct position.

### T01.GK.06
**Title:** What comes next?
**Dependencies:** T01.GK.01
**Description:** Look at the first two pictures. What should come last? MCQ, choose final picture.

### T01.GK.07
**Title:** Find the pattern that repeats
**Dependencies:** T01.GK.01
**Description:** Watch the actions. Which two actions happen over and over? Example: hop-clap-hop-clap. Visual pattern MCQ.

### T01.GK.08
**Title:** Count how many times
**Dependencies:** T01.GK.07
**Description:** Watch the character perform a repeated action (e.g., jump, clap, spin). Count how many times by selecting from picture-based answer choices. Short animation (2-4 seconds) + picture-based count choices showing 1-4 actions.

**Title:** Fix one picture that is out of order
**Dependencies:** T02.GK.03

### T03.GK.03

**Dependencies:** T01.GK.01

### T03.GK.04
**Title:** Choose the missing middle step in a routine
**Dependencies:** T03.GK.03
**Description:** Students see the first and last steps of a 3-step routine and choose the picture that fits in the middle so the plan for the task makes sense.

### T03.GK.05
**Title:** Describe what each step accomplishes
**Dependencies:** T03.GK.03
**Description:** Students look at each step in a familiar routine and select or describe what it does or what changes after that step (e.g., "After washing hands, hands are clean").

## T06 - Events & Sequences (3 skills)

### T06.GK.01
**Title:** Order pictures showing a morning routine (event sequence concept)
**Dependencies:** T01.GK.01
**Description:** Students arrange 3-4 picture cards showing a simple morning routine in the correct order (e.g., wake up → brush teeth → eat breakfast → go to school). This unplugged activity builds foundational understanding of event sequences and chronological ordering without coding.

### T06.GK.02
**Title:** Match "first" and "next" words to pictures in a sequence
**Dependencies:** T06.GK.01
**Description:** Students match the words "first," "next," and "last" to pictures in a simple 3-step sequence (e.g., planting a seed: first dig hole, next put seed in, last cover with dirt). This introduces sequencing vocabulary essential for understanding event-driven programming concepts.

### T06.GK.03
**Title:** Predict what happens "after this" in a picture sequence
**Dependencies:** T06.GK.02
**Description:** Given 2-3 pictures showing the start of a familiar sequence, students choose what picture comes next from 2-3 options (e.g., "If you put on your shoes, what happens next?"). This develops cause-and-effect thinking and prediction skills foundational to understanding event triggers.

## T08 - Conditions & Logic (2 skills)

### T08.GK.01
**Title:** Match pictures to "if it rains" rules
**Dependencies:** None
**Description:** Students look at pictures showing weather and actions. Match the picture to the correct "If it rains, then..." sentence. For example, match umbrella picture to "If it rains, then use an umbrella." This is a drag-and-drop matching activity with 3-4 items that introduces the concept of conditional rules using familiar, concrete examples.

### T08.GK.02
**Title:** Choose what happens next based on yes/no
**Dependencies:** T08.GK.01
**Description:** Students look at a picture and a yes/no question, then choose what happens next. For example, "Is the light green?" If yes, walk. If no, wait. This is a multiple-choice question with 2 picture choices that builds understanding of binary decision-making.

## T10 - Lists & Tables (8 skills)

### T10.GK.01
**Title:** Sort picture cards into groups
**Dependencies:** None
**Description:** Students sort 4-6 picture cards into 2-3 groups based on a visible attribute (color, shape, or type). For example: sort animals into "pets" and "wild animals," or sort shapes into "circles" and "not circles." This builds the foundational concept of categorization.

### T10.GK.02
**Title:** Count items in each group
**Dependencies:** T10.GK.01
**Description:** After sorting items into groups, students count how many items are in each group and select the count from picture-based answer choices (showing 1, 2, 3, or 4 dots/items).

### T10.GK.03
**Title:** Find which group has more
**Dependencies:** T10.GK.02
**Description:** Students compare two groups of sorted items and identify which group has more items. Answer choices show pictures of the two groups.

### T10.GK.04
**Title:** Add an item to the right group
**Dependencies:** T10.GK.01
**Description:** Students are shown sorted groups and one new item. They decide which group the new item belongs to and drag it there.

### T10.GK.05
**Title:** Find the first and last item in a row
**Dependencies:** T01.GK.03
**Description:** Students identify the first and last item in a row of 3-5 picture cards arranged in order (left to right).

### T10.GK.06
**Title:** Look at a simple picture table
**Dependencies:** T10.GK.01
**Description:** Students view a simple 2x3 or 3x3 picture table (e.g., a chart showing which child likes which fruit) and answer questions like "What does Sam like?" by tapping the correct cell.

### T10.GK.07
**Title:** Match items that go together
**Dependencies:** T10.GK.01
**Description:** Students match pairs of related items (e.g., match animals to their homes, match tools to their uses) by drawing lines or dragging.

### T10.GK.08
**Title:** Find all items with a special mark
**Dependencies:** T10.GK.02
**Description:** Students identify all items in a collection that have a special visual mark (star, checkmark, red border) and count them.

## T13 - Testing, Debugging & Error Handling (3 skills)

### T13.GK.01
**Title:** Spot a missing or wrong action in an animation
**Dependencies:** None
**Description:** Students watch a short picture story or animation and compare what happens to what was supposed to happen (e.g., the character should jump but doesn't, or moves the wrong direction). They simply point to or circle the moment something went wrong without explaining why or fixing it.

### T13.GK.02
**Title:** Retry after noticing something went wrong
**Dependencies:** T01.GK.01, T13.GK.01
**Description:** Students follow a simple set of picture steps for a task (e.g., stacking blocks, moving a character on a board), notice when the result is not what was intended, and try the steps again from the beginning. They experience the "try, check, retry" cycle without changing the steps themselves.

### T13.GK.03
**Title:** Fix a single wrong direction or action in steps
**Dependencies:** T01.GK.03, T13.GK.01
**Description:** Students "play computer" by acting out or watching a character follow a short list of picture steps to reach a fun goal (like a treasure or door). When the character ends up in the wrong place or does something silly, they identify which one movement is wrong (e.g., an arrow pointing left instead of right, or "sit down" too early) and swap it for a better step so the routine works.
