### 1. T35.G5.02 - Debate digital well-being scenarios
**OLD:** "Students debate policy scenarios (device-free times, notifications) referencing evidence on focus and health."

**NEW:** Expanded to detailed evidence-based debate process with:
- Research evidence on screen time effects (2-3 articles/studies)
- Prepare debate positions on specific policy scenarios
- Conduct structured debates with evidence points
- Document arguments and reach evidence-based conclusions

### 2. T35.G6.04 - Examine digital divide data
**OLD:** "Learners interpret charts (broadband availability, device ownership) and propose community actions."

**NEW:** Expanded to comprehensive data analysis and action proposal process with:
- Interpret 2-3 data visualizations on digital access disparities
- Identify root causes of disparities (infrastructure, economic, literacy, awareness)
- Propose 2-3 specific, realistic community actions with evidence
- Present proposals with supporting evidence and implementation plans

### 3. T35.G8.01.01 - Analyze AI chatbots' impact on information literacy
**OLD:** Generic description mentioning research habits, critical thinking, misinformation, and equity

**NEW:** Expanded to comprehensive analysis with 5 specific components:
- Research habits analysis (surveys, before/after comparisons)
- Critical thinking impact (fact-checking, recognizing limitations)
- Misinformation risk (testing accuracy, identifying patterns)
- Educational equity impact (comparing across student groups)
- Develop responsible use guidelines (specific, evidence-based)
