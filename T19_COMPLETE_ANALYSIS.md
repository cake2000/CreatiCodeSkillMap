### **Phase 2: Block Coding Introduction (G3) ‚Äì 16 skills**

Grade 3 represents the **critical transition** from unplugged to coded art. Skills split into two parallel tracks:

**Track A: Pen Blocks (drawing trails)**
- T19.G3.03-06: pen down, pen up, color, size
- T19.G3.06.01: erase all

**Track B: Looks Draw Blocks (shapes at positions)**
- T19.G3.07-08: draw rectangle, draw oval

**Integration:**
- T19.G3.01: Translating unplugged recipes to blocks (bridge skill)
- T19.G3.02 & .02.01: Understanding when to use Pen vs Looks
- T19.G3.09-11: Loops for borders, grids, nested patterns
- T19.G3.12-13: Adding randomness and variables
- T19.G3.14: Debugging visual output

**Key Strength:** Clear pedagogical sequence‚Äîstudents learn each drawing system separately, then combine them with loops. The comparison between Pen and Looks is explicit, preventing confusion.

   - Visual-heavy curriculum may challenge visually impaired students

   - Could expand tactile/audio art options in early grades

6. **Assessment Challenges**
   - Art is subjective‚Äîhow do you assess "successful" generative art?
   - T19.G5.12 (explain design choices) and T19.G8.03 (evaluate authorship) address this
